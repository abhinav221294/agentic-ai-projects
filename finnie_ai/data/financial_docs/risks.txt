Title: Investment Risk Guide
Source: Finnie AI Knowledge Base

What is Investment Risk?

Investment risk refers to the possibility of losing money or not achieving expected returns when investing in financial assets like stocks, mutual funds, or bonds. All investments carry some level of risk.

How Investment Risk Works
Investors put money into financial assets.
Market conditions and external factors affect asset performance.
Prices of investments may rise or fall over time.
Returns may be higher or lower than expected.
In some cases, investors may lose part or all of their invested capital.
Types of Investment Risk
Market Risk

The risk of losses due to overall market movements.
Affects stocks, mutual funds, and other market-linked investments.

Credit Risk

The risk that a borrower or issuer may fail to repay their debt.
Common in bonds and debt funds.

Liquidity Risk

The risk of not being able to sell an investment quickly without losing value.

Inflation Risk

The risk that rising prices reduce the purchasing power of returns.

Interest Rate Risk

The risk that changes in interest rates affect the value of investments, especially bonds.

Benefits of Understanding Risk

Better Decision Making: Helps investors choose suitable investments.
Risk Management: Enables diversification and planning.
Goal Alignment: Aligns investments with financial goals and risk tolerance.

What is Risk Tolerance?

Risk tolerance is the level of risk an investor is willing and able to take. It depends on factors like income, financial goals, age, and investment horizon.

Risks Involved

No investment is completely risk-free.
Higher returns usually come with higher risk.
Market conditions can be unpredictable.

Example

If an investor buys a stock at Rs. 100 and the price falls to Rs. 70, there is a loss of Rs. 30.
If the price rises to Rs. 130, there is a gain of Rs. 30.

Final Thought

Understanding investment risk is essential for making informed decisions. A balanced approach, including diversification and long-term planning, can help manage risk effectively.
