Title: Stocks (Equity Investment) Guide
Source: Finnie AI Knowledge Base

What are Stocks?

Stocks represent ownership in a company. When you buy a stock, you own a small portion of that company and are called a shareholder. The value of your investment depends on the company’s performance and market conditions.

How Stocks Work
Companies issue stocks to raise money.
Investors buy these stocks through stock exchanges.
Stock prices change based on demand, supply, and company performance.
Investors can earn returns through price appreciation or dividends.
Stocks can be bought or sold at any time during market hours.
Types of Stocks
Common Stocks

These give ownership in a company and voting rights.
Returns come from price growth and dividends.

Preferred Stocks

These provide fixed dividends but usually do not offer voting rights.
They are less risky than common stocks.

Growth Stocks

These belong to companies expected to grow rapidly.
They usually do not pay dividends but offer high return potential.

Dividend Stocks

These provide regular dividend income along with moderate growth.

Benefits of Stocks

High Return Potential: Stocks can generate higher returns compared to other investments.
Ownership: Investors become partial owners of a company.
Liquidity: Stocks can be easily bought and sold in the market.
Dividend Income: Some stocks provide regular income through dividends.

What is a Stock Exchange?

A stock exchange is a marketplace where stocks are bought and sold. Examples include NSE (National Stock Exchange) and BSE (Bombay Stock Exchange). Investors trade stocks through brokers or trading platforms.

Risks Involved

Market Risk: Stock prices can fluctuate due to market conditions.
Company Risk: Poor company performance can reduce stock value.
Volatility: Prices can change rapidly in the short term.
No Guaranteed Returns: Profits are not assured.

Example

If you buy 10 shares of a company at Rs. 100 each, your total investment is Rs. 1000.
If the price rises to Rs. 120, your investment becomes Rs. 1200.
If the price falls to Rs. 80, your investment becomes Rs. 800.

Final Thought

Stocks are a powerful investment option for wealth creation but come with higher risk. They are suitable for investors who can tolerate market fluctuations and are willing to invest for the long term.
