What is Inflation?

Inflation is the rate at which the general level of prices for goods and services increases over time. As inflation rises, the purchasing power of money decreases, meaning you can buy fewer goods and services with the same amount of money.

How Inflation Works
Prices of goods and services increase over time.
The value of money decreases as purchasing power falls.
Central banks monitor and try to control inflation.
Moderate inflation is normal in a growing economy.
High inflation can reduce savings and increase the cost of living.
Types of Inflation
Demand-Pull Inflation

Occurs when demand for goods and services exceeds supply.

Cost-Push Inflation

Occurs when production costs increase, leading to higher prices.

Built-In Inflation

Occurs due to rising wages, which increase production costs and prices.

Benefits of Inflation

Encourages Spending: People tend to spend or invest rather than hold cash.
Supports Economic Growth: Moderate inflation is a sign of a growing economy.

What is Purchasing Power?

Purchasing power refers to the amount of goods and services that can be bought with a unit of money. Inflation reduces purchasing power over time.

Risks Involved

Reduced Savings Value: Money loses value over time.
Higher Cost of Living: Everyday expenses increase.
Uncertainty: High inflation can create economic instability.

Example

If a product costs Rs. 100 today and inflation is 5% annually, the same product may cost Rs. 105 next year.
This means the value of money has decreased.

Final Thought

Inflation is an important economic factor that affects investments and daily life. Understanding inflation helps investors choose strategies that protect and grow their wealth over time.
